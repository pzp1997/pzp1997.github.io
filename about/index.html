About

Palmer Paul

Welcome!

Hey there! Welcome to my site! My name is Palmer, and I am currently studying computer science as an undergradutate student in the School of Engineering and Applied Science at the University of Pennsylvania in Philadelphia, PA. I am very proficient in many programming languages, and I guess you could say that I have a knack for programming. At the moment, I am just trying to broaden my horizons as much as possible by working on various projects, which can be found on my GitHub. Since coming to Penn, I have also become quite interested in the theoretical side of computer science, and lately, have been writing mathematical proofs both for courses and for fun.

Beginnings

My programming career started in 2012 when my school's Engineering Club was building a rudimentary 3D printer, and we needed someone to design some software for it. Despite being a high school freshman and having little to no prior programming experience, I decided to take on the challenge. To suit up for the task, I started learning Python. After completing that project I knew I had caught the coding bug, and went on to teach myself JavaScript, Processing, Java (including the whole AP Computer Science curriculum), and a slew of other programming languages.

Future

I am excited to announce that I will be working for the Elm Software Foundation this summer as part of Google Summer of Code! Elm is a functional programming language for creating web apps. My project is to explore the possibility of extending Elm for mobile app development. You can read more about it on the GSoC website!

Present

Starting in Spring 2017, I became a TA for one of Penn's intro computer science courses, Programming Languages and Techniques I (CIS 120). My responsibilities include leading a weekly recitation, holding weekly office hours, and grading homeworks and exams.

The computer science courses that I am currently enrolled in are Programming Languages and Techniques II (CIS 121), which focuses on data structures and algorithms, and Automata, Computability, and Complexity (CIS 262).

In the past semester (Fall 2016), I was enrolled in two computer science courses: Programming Languages and Techniques I (CIS 120) and Mathematical Foundations of Computer Science (CIS 160). CIS 120, which is taught in OCaml and Java, taught me about abstraction and modularity, test-driven development, and how to implement basic data structures. In CIS 160, I learned graph theory, set theory, and combinatorics, as well as how to write rigorous mathematical proofs.

Past

Last summer, I interned at Sefaria as a software engineer. The most prominent contribution I made was the export to drive feature for source sheets. You can read about it here.

Last year, I wrote a handful of ScratchX extensions, including a geolocation extension!

In the summer of 2015, I experimented with computer vision using the OpenCV library. My primary project was finding an effective method of determining the gender of a person in real-time using full body images (i.e. no face detection). This project is still a work in progress.

During my junior year of high school, I created a Content Management System (CMS) with a friend of mine in Computer Programming Club. I wrote the entire backend using Node.js (Express and MongoDB), as well as a good portion of the frontend using Jade (a nifty little HTML templating language), CSS, and JavaScript with jQuery.
